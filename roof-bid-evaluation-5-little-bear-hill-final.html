04

Recommendation

Memo

Go with Express Roofing at $17,095. It's ~$2,100 cheaper than a matched Apex, installs the higher-spec solar skylights, and carries the longer warranty. Choose Apex instead only if its credential and review record are worth the premium to you.

Why Express

On the matched scope it wins on several fronts: lower price, solar skylights vs venting, a 50-year workmanship warranty against Apex's 25, explicit garage coverage, and a confirmed discount. The roofing systems themselves (OC vs CertainTeed architectural, 6-nail/130 mph) are equivalent in quality tier.

The case for paying more for Apex

Apex is the stronger outfit on paper: a CertainTeed ShingleMaster Premier credential that backs its warranty, the owner on every job, and a near-flawless record across 271+ reviews vs. Express's strong-but-spottier history. If a flawless install and the cleanest track record matter more to you than ~$2,100 and a solar upgrade, Apex is the defensible splurge.

Before you sign

  • Express: get the $1,000 discount and the exact solar skylight models written onto the signed proposal; confirm the 6/29 timeline works for you.
  • Apex: confirm the $2,100 venting price in writing, whether the garage is in scope, and how interior skylight trim is handled.
  • Both: ask what board replacement they expect — sheathing/plywood is the likeliest surprise on the final invoice.

Both firms are well-reviewed; sentiment is the weakest of the five signals. The numbers point to Express — let your read of each owner settle any remaining doubt.
