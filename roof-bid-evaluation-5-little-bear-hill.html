04

Recommendation

Memo

Lean Express Roofing as the value choice — but confirm the coupon first. If install-quality certainty matters more than ~$2,500–$3,500, Apex is the safe premium alternative.

Why Express edges it

On equal-weighted scoring it finishes first (4.3 vs 4.2). It's the lower bid by ~$2,500 (more with the coupon), carries a 50-year workmanship warranty against Apex's 25, and is the only bid that explicitly names the garage. The work scope and OC system are comparable to Apex's.

Why it's close

Apex brings the stronger quality signals: a CertainTeed ShingleMaster Premier credential (which backs its warranty), the owner on every job, and a near-flawless review record across 271+ ratings. Its proposal is also the more buttoned-up document. None of that is cheap insurance — it's worth roughly the price premium if a flawless install and the cleanest paper trail are what you value most.

Before you sign

  • Call Express about the $1,000 coupon. The 6/15 deadline has passed — get written confirmation they'll still honor it, or treat their price as $12,495.
  • Confirm the garage is in Apex's scope in writing if you go that way ("all roof sections of home" is ambiguous).
  • Ask both for a likely board-replacement estimate. Sheathing/plywood is the line item most likely to change the final cost.
  • Note Express's late-payment terms (2.5%/mo + collection fees). A non-issue if you pay at completion, but worth knowing.

Sentiment is one of six dimensions and the weakest signal of the set — both firms are well-reviewed, so let the contract substance and your own gut from meeting each owner carry the final call.

05

How this was scored

  1. Extraction. Scope, cost, discounts, materials, timeline, warranty, and terms were pulled from each contract PDF.
  2. Six dimensions, equal weight. Scope coverage, description thoroughness, approach quality, public sentiment, cost, and discount/value — each 1–5, averaged into the composite.
  3. Relative cost. Cost and value are scored against each other, not an absolute scale. With only two bids the strict low=5/high=1 formula is degenerate, so the ~$2,500–3,500 gap was scored on judgment and stated as such.
  4. Evidence-backed. Every score points to a contract line or a cited review. Sentiment came from web search; where a vendor's public footprint is thin it's flagged rather than guessed.
  5. Coverage beats price. A cheaper bid that drops scope is penalized on coverage so it can't win on cost alone.
